# Logo concepts — Andrew Malone Design

Six candidate marks to replace the current peak-and-wave-in-a-circle logo.
All six are drawn in the site's warm ink (`#1C1A17` from `src/styles/tokens.css`),
work at 16 px favicon size, and ship as standalone SVGs. Swap the fill/stroke to
`currentColor` (or `#F6F4EE`) for use on dark grounds.

| # | File | Concept |
|---|------|---------|
| 01 | `01-ridgeline.svg` | Closest evolution of the current mark: peak (A) over wave (m), no enclosing circle, two round strokes. |
| 02 | `02-tile.svg` | Favicon-first: the peak knocked out of a solid rounded square. Carries its own ground, so it never dissolves against a tab bar. The knockout is a true mask, not a painted line. |
| 03 | `03-assembly.svg` | Initials as a geometry kit — a triangle for the A, a dome for the m, on a shared baseline. The most abstract option. |
| 04 | `04-fold.svg` | One strip folded into a peak; the lighter return face (55 % opacity of the same ink) gives depth without a second color. |
| 05 | `05-am.svg` | Lowercase typographic mark, `am.`, drawn as geometry rather than typed in a font. Doubles as a case-study sign-off. |
| 06 | `06-daybreak.svg` | A solid sun crossed by the original wave: A.M. — literally morning. Keeps the circular equity of the old logo, flipped from outline to solid. |

## Round two — riffs on Tile (02) and Daybreak (06)

Tile won on ground (it owns its shape in a tab bar); Daybreak won on story
(sun + wave, A.M.). These ten explore the space between them.

| # | File | Concept |
|---|------|---------|
| 07 | `07-tideline.svg` | The direct hybrid: Daybreak's horizon wave cut through Tile's rounded square. |
| 08 | `08-emblem.svg` | The full A/M story in the tile: peak above, wave below. Densest, most "app icon." |
| 09 | `09-orb.svg` | Tile's peak knockout in a solid circle — the original circle resurrected, outline flipped to solid. |
| 10 | `10-notch.svg` | The mountain cut into the tile from below; ink is the sky, paper is the peak. |
| 11 | `11-counter.svg` | A triangular A cut from the tile with its counter floating inside — the most letterform. |
| 12 | `12-eclipse.svg` | Daybreak with the wave at the equator: an even dome/bowl split, boldest silhouette. |
| 13 | `13-ripple.svg` | Two waves through the sun; three bands, the m speaks as loudly as the sun. |
| 14 | `14-rise.svg` | Uncontained: the sun rising behind a free horizon wave, lower edge cut by the waterline. |
| 15 | `15-summit.svg` | The peak in front of the morning sun, separated by a sliver of paper. Most pictorial. |
| 16 | `16-meridian.svg` | A wide half-sun rising through the wave. Low, horizontal, calm — header-first. |
